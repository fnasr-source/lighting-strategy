Executive Diagnosis

  • The previous motion generated reach, but not enough conversion. The older report showed 1,669 total sends under a broad multi-persona approach; the live active stack now shows 1,000 sends with only 1 reply and 52 bounces.
  • The problem is not only lead volume. The core issue is that the message has been too generic, the wedge has been too broad, and the funnel has lacked a proof-driven conversion layer.
  • Cold outbound has been carrying too much of the load alone. There was no disciplined multi-channel follow-up motion for the high-intent education leads already sitting in the CRM.
  • The best near-term path is to optimize for booked pilot scoping calls, not raw reach, and to focus first on education buyers in Egypt, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE.

Reset Principles

Education-first Booked demos first MENA priority Multi-channel No blind scaling

This reset treats the CRM as the real conversion layer, keeps weaker outreach lanes capped, and builds the next 30 days around one offer: a 30-day hands-on cyber lab pilot with measurable outcomes.

Why Results Stalled

  • The earlier report was right about execution breadth, but the market message stayed too broad: hosted labs, CTFs, training, and enterprise security were all being pushed in parallel.
  • The funnel optimized for sends instead of for a measurable conversion event like a booked demo or pilot scoping call.
  • Warm leads existed in Asana, but there was no enforced owner/next-action discipline.
  • There was no dedicated education proof layer: no pilot brief, no outcomes framework, no landing page focused on institutional adoption, and no recovery sequence for warm records.
  • The legacy campaigns also carried unhealthy bounce rates, especially on the older CTF lane, which made blind scaling a bad decision.

New Strategic Direction

Primary ICP

  • Universities and higher-education institutions
  • Cybersecurity instructors and faculty leads
  • Program directors, deans, and training heads
  • Education-linked innovation and skills units

Core Offer

  • 30-day hands-on cyber lab pilot
  • Faculty/instructor enablement
  • Browser-based isolated labs with no DevOps burden
  • Measurable learner engagement and readiness

Commercial CTA

  • One CTA only: book a pilot scoping call
  • Use pilot outline and outcomes framework as conversion support
  • Keep security and CTF lanes live only as secondary tests until education proves repeatable

The positioning shift is from “a platform with features” to “the fastest way for education teams to launch measurable hands-on cyber learning without infrastructure overhead.”

  • Create a dedicated education pilot page built around one institution-level promise and one CTA.
  • Support cold outreach with proof assets: pilot brief, outcomes framework, proof sheet, and a short demo/pilot deck.

  • Outcome proof: engagement, completion, readiness, and faculty efficiency.
