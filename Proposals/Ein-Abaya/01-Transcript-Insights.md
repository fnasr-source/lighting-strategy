# 01-Transcript-Insights: Ein Abaya Discovery to Kickoff

**CONFIDENTIAL | Prepared by: Admireworks**  
**Primary Client Contact:** Awatif Madawi  
**Known Marketing Stakeholder:** Muhannad Allazzam  
**Coverage Window:** January 21, 2026 to March 1, 2026

---

## 1. What The Client Actually Wants
- The immediate priority is the **website/store rebuild**.
- The broader opportunity is a **conversion-led growth system**, not just cosmetic design work.
- The team wants the store to better reflect the brand’s product experience, storytelling, and trust level.
- They do not want to disrupt current sales momentum while the new store is being built.

## 2. Brand Positioning Clarified
- **Ein Abaya** is not being positioned as a niche ultra-luxury-only label.
- Awatif described it as a brand that can be worn by a university student and also by a deputy minister.
- The core promise is elevated quality and elegance with broader reach than a pure couture line.
- **Maison Ein** is the higher-end line: upgraded materials, higher price point, more elevated positioning, but still benefiting from trust already built under Ein Abaya.
- Dresses are part of the expansion direction, not only abayas.

## 3. Why The Website Matters
- The client repeatedly emphasized that the current store experience is not strong enough.
- Admireworks identified weak points in:
  - product storytelling
  - social proof visibility
  - conversion-focused product pages
  - welcome-offer speed and follow-up
  - trust signals for first-time visitors
- The website should feel closer to a premium lookbook plus direct-response selling environment, not just a static catalog.

## 4. Channel Reality From The Meetings
- **Instagram / Meta** is the strongest current channel.
- **TikTok** is active and strategically important, but results are inconsistent relative to Instagram.
- **Google** was tested but not maintained because results were weaker than other channels at the time.
- **Snapchat** was tested and was not considered strong at that stage.
- **WhatsApp** is active in the customer journey, but welcome automation is currently slower than ideal.

## 5. Commercial / Delivery Direction
- Early discussions framed the work as website optimization plus broader growth support.
- By March 1, the proposal being discussed included:
  - website rebuild
  - marketing plan / 90-day strategy
  - tracking setup
  - first-month support
- The client and team were still evaluating platform choice in those calls.
- The final update from the user is now clear: **the project starts on Zid**.

## 6. What The Team Needs From The Client
- Zid access
- domain / DNS ownership
- payment gateway and BNPL status
- shipping and logistics rules
- analytics and ads access
- policy links
- brand and media folders
- product export / catalog files
- final approver details

## 7. Strategic Takeaway
This is not a generic store migration. The right positioning is:

**A Zid rebuild for a premium modest-fashion brand where conversion rate, trust, product experience, and brand ladder clarity matter as much as the visual design itself.**
