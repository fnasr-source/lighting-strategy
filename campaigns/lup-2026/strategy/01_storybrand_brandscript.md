# StoryBrand BrandScript — Leading Under Pressure 2026

## One-Liner
**Most leaders are never trained for the moments that matter most. Our 3-day executive program, designed by IMD and Stanford faculty, equips you with the crisis leadership tools to lead with clarity when the pressure is highest.**

---

## 1. A CHARACTER (The Hero)

**Who they are:** A senior executive (C-suite, VP, GM, Regional Director) leading teams of 50+ people in complex, high-stakes environments — energy, finance, healthcare, government, or multinational corporations.

**What they want:**
- The ability to make decisive, confident decisions under extreme pressure
- Tools and frameworks to navigate crises — not just survive them, but lead through them
- Recognition as a resilient, composed leader who elevates performance when it matters most

**Identity aspiration:** "I want to be the leader people turn to when everything is falling apart."

---

## 2. HAS A PROBLEM

### External Problem (Tangible)
"I face high-pressure decisions — restructuring, market disruptions, regulatory crises, boardroom conflicts — and I don't have a proven crisis leadership playbook."

### Internal Problem (Emotional)
"Deep down, I'm not sure I have what it takes to stay calm and lead effectively when everything is on the line. The weight of this responsibility is lonely."

### Philosophical Problem (Moral/Aspirational)
"Leaders shouldn't have to learn crisis management through failure. There should be a way to build this capacity proactively, before the crisis hits."

### The Villain
**Decision paralysis, reactive leadership, and fear-driven management** — the silent killers of organizational trust, talent retention, and strategic opportunity during turbulent times.

## 4. WHO GIVES THEM A PLAN

### The 3-Step Plan

**Step 1 — ASSESS (6 Weeks Before)**
Complete a confidential leadership-under-pressure self-assessment. Identify your stress patterns, decision-making blind spots, and resilience baseline. Arrive knowing exactly where to focus.

**Step 2 — IMMERSE (3 Days in Dubai)**
Dive into VR crisis simulations, real-world case studies from Special Forces to Silicon Valley, and peer-learning with 25 senior leaders. Practice making high-stakes decisions in a safe-to-fail environment with world-class faculty coaching you in real time.

**Step 3 — INTEGRATE (6 Weeks After)**
Leave with a personal action plan and a 1-hour coaching session to embed your new crisis leadership tools into daily practice. Transform what you learned into how you lead — permanently.

## Messaging Guidelines

### Tone of Voice
| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Authoritative** | We speak from proven expertise, not theory |
| **Empathetic** | We acknowledge the real emotional weight of leadership |
| **Urgent** | Time-sensitive, limited seats, the pressure is real and current |
| **Aspirational** | We paint a picture of the leader they can become |
| **Direct** | No fluff — every word earns its place |

### Key Phrases to Use
- "When the pressure is highest, your leadership matters most"
- "From Special Forces to the boardroom"
- "The crisis leadership playbook you wish you had"
- "Lead with clarity, not fear"
- "Pressure reveals character. This program shapes it."
- "The only program that combines VR crisis simulation with IMD and Stanford faculty"

### Phrases to Avoid
- "Interesting" / "Exciting" (too generic)
- "Learn about" (passive — use "Master" / "Build" / "Develop")
- "Best practices" (overused — use "proven frameworks" / "battle-tested tools")
- "Webinar" or "Online course" (this is an in-person, high-touch experience)
