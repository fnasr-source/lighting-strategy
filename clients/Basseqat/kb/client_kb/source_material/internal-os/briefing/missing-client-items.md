# Missing Client Items To Request

This list is based on what the standard briefing requires but the client PDF does not fully answer.

## High Priority

- Official website URL or current landing page URL.
- Instagram handle and any other active social media accounts.
- Confirmed main contact person for the project.
- Main contact email address.
- Main contact phone number and preferred WhatsApp number.
- Clear company location details:
  legal base, operating base, and target markets.
- Short company origin story and mission statement.
- Explicit customer-facing articulation of the main problem the offer solves.
- Named competitors or alternative offers the client is actively compared against.
- Any existing slogan or tagline in English or Arabic.
- Actual testimonials, case studies, proof assets, or customer success stories.
- Links to those proof assets if they are stored on Google Drive.
- Brand manual, visual guidelines, or the referenced social brief.
- Actual brand guardrails and visual-direction files mentioned in the PDF.
- Farm images and videos available for use.
- Legal references or approved wording that can be used publicly.
- Sales FAQs and scripts currently used by the team.

## Needed To Complete The Questionnaire Properly

- Formal persona count confirmation.
- Persona A label in the client’s own language.
- Persona B label in the client’s own language.
- Persona B age range.
- Persona B interests and motivations.
- Persona B main concerns or objections.
- Which channels the audience already uses and trusts most.
- Current product or offer categories, if there is more than one.
- Which product or offer is most popular right now and why.
- Current incentives or offers:
  discounts, bonuses, visits, webinars, brochures, or other conversion drivers.
- Current marketing and sales stack:
  Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, TikTok Ads, CRM, analytics tools, WhatsApp tools, email tools, ecommerce platform, and anything else in use.
- Which past marketing strategies have worked well before.

## Helpful But Not Strictly Blocking

- Full list of online presence:
  website, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, WhatsApp entry points, and any listing pages.
- Historical campaign data, if any exists.
- Examples of common objections heard by the sales team.
- Existing brochure, webinar, visit-request flow, or virtual tour materials.
- Clarification on whether there is one core offer only or multiple offer variants.

## Immediate Follow-Up Note

The PDF says the client will provide several inputs later, but they are not actually included in the document. Those should be requested explicitly rather than assumed to already exist in the project:

- brand guardrails
- core messaging
- reserved word and tone rules
- visual direction
- farm images and videos
- proof assets
- legal references
- sales FAQs and scripts
