# Copy Review Rubric

Use this rubric before approving any client-facing copy.

## Scoring
- `approve`: strong enough to use with light polish only
- `revise`: direction is usable but important weaknesses remain
- `reject`: the copy is not reliable and should not be used

## 1. Source Truth
- Are the core claims supported by source material?
- Are inferences separated from confirmed facts?
- Does the copy avoid invented certainty?

Reject if the copy depends on made-up facts or fake confidence.

## 2. Audience Fit
- Is the copy clearly written for one main buyer?
- Does it reflect the buyer's real stakes and hesitations?
- Is the awareness stage clear?

Reject if the copy sounds like it is trying to address everyone.

## 3. Market-Native Language
- Does the language sound like a real writer from this market wrote it?
- Does Arabic avoid translated English structure?
- Does English avoid startup cliche and agency filler?

Reject if the phrasing feels translated, stiff, or generic.

## 4. Offer Clarity
- Is the offer easy to understand?
- Is the promised result concrete?
- Does the copy make clear why this offer matters now?

Reject if the buyer still would not know what they are being asked to consider.

## 5. Proof Support
- Is each important claim backed by proof or appropriately softened?
- Is proof attached to the right claims?
- Is the proof specific enough to increase trust?

Reject if the copy asks for trust without evidence.

## 6. Objection Coverage
- Are the major objections named or handled?
- Is the handling credible instead of defensive?
- Does the flow reduce friction at the right moment?

Reject if obvious hesitation is ignored.

## 7. CTA Fit
- Is there one main CTA?
- Does it fit the stage of awareness?
- Does it fit the channel?

Reject if the CTA is vague, premature, or mismatched to the asset.

## 8. Channel Fit
- Does the asset behave like its channel should?
- Does ad copy stay fast and angle-led?
- Does WhatsApp stay human and low-friction?
- Does landing-page copy carry the full argument?

Reject if the copy could be swapped into a different channel without change.

## 9. Human Quality
- Does it sound like an experienced copywriter wrote it?
- Is the rhythm natural?
- Are there sharp, memorable lines without fake intensity?
- Does it avoid obvious AI filler?

Reject if it reads like a template or generated summary.

## 10. Final Decision Notes
Every review should end with:
- decision: approve / revise / reject
- top 3 issues
- missing proof or confirmations
- exact next revision target
