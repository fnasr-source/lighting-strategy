# English Direct-Response Angle Example

## Context
- Category: B2B offer
- Buyer state: problem-aware, time-poor, skeptical of vague promises
- Goal: produce angle-led ads, not random variants

## Angle 1: Hidden Cost
### Hook
Your team is not slow. Your workflow is leaking time in places nobody owns.

### Body
Most operators do not need more tools.
They need one clear process for what happens before work stalls, after work stalls, and when nobody follows up.

### CTA
See the operating breakdown

### Why it works
- Sharp tension.
- Useful specificity without fake numbers.
- CTA matches a problem-aware audience.

## Angle 2: Proof-Led
### Hook
If the process only works when one person pushes every task, it is not a system.

### Body
The fix is not motivation.
It is a visible operating rhythm, tracked handoffs, and fewer invisible decisions.

### CTA
Book a working session

### Why it works
- Strong reframe.
- Speaks like an operator, not a marketer.
- CTA fits warmer, more solution-aware traffic.

## Angle 3: Objection Handling
### Hook
No, you do not need a massive rebuild to fix client delivery chaos.

### Body
You need the fewest changes that remove the most friction:
- clearer intake
- cleaner handoffs
- one decision path for approvals

### CTA
See what to change first

### Why it works
- Enters through a real objection.
- Makes the solution feel manageable.
- Reduces fear before pushing conversion.
