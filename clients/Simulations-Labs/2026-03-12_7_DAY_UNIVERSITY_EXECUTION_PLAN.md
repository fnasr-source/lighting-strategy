# Simulations Labs - 7 Day University Execution Plan

- Plan date: 2026-03-12
- Time horizon: 7 days
- Primary objective: turn the education focus into booked university calls and one early proof path

## Success Criteria
- 3-5 qualified university conversations advanced
- 1-2 discovery or pilot scoping calls booked
- 1 prioritized Apollo lead pool built and approved
- existing education leads fully followed up with dated next steps
- one clean outbound narrative used consistently across email and call prep

## Day 1
### Lock the operating focus
- Confirm the working ICP is university-focused only for this sprint.
- Use three lead buckets only:
  - university decision makers
  - faculty / instructors / lab operators
  - warm education recovery leads already in CRM
- Freeze non-core outreach expansion for this 7-day window unless it directly supports education.

### Immediate CRM cleanup
- Review the current education hot/warm queue first:
  - Rabab Nabawy
  - Ahmed Elgyar
  - Ayman
  - Sameh Ayman
  - any other university or training-program contacts in follow-up
- Make sure each has one dated next action and one owner.

## Day 2
### Build the fresh Apollo university pool
- Pull fresh university leads using the Apollo brief in `2026-03-12_APOLLO_UNIVERSITY_TARGETING_BRIEF.md`.
- Keep the first pass small and clean:
  - 60-100 decision makers
  - 60-100 faculty / instructors
  - 20-40 training-program or lab operators
- Prioritize relevance over total count.

### Priority geographies
- Tier 1: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, UAE
- Tier 2: UK, US
- Tier 3 if volume is weak: Netherlands, Germany

## Day 3
### Tighten the outbound narrative
- Standardize one education offer:
  - 30-day hands-on cyber lab pilot
  - one cohort
  - one use case
  - measurable outcomes
  - no internal infrastructure burden
- Use one CTA only:
  - book a pilot scoping call

### Supporting assets to use
- education pilot brief
- outcomes framework
- education proof sheet

## Day 4
### Launch the first university-only outbound batch
- Start with the cleanest 30-50 leads, not the full list.
- Split by role:
  - decision maker messaging
  - faculty / operator messaging
- Do not mix university leads with general security or CTF organizer messaging.

## Day 5
### Multi-channel follow-up on replies and known warm leads
- Review every reply manually.
- For any positive signal, move to:
  - proof sheet
  - pilot brief
  - call scheduling
- Add LinkedIn touchpoints or WhatsApp only where there is already context or warm signal.

## Day 6
### Measure signal quality, not just activity
- Check:
  - replies
  - positive replies
  - booked calls
  - bounce rate
  - strongest titles / institutions responding
- Kill weak filters quickly if they are producing noise.

## Day 7
### Decide what scales next
- Keep only the segments, titles, and geographies producing useful replies.
- Promote the best-performing university filter into the default Apollo play.
- Write a short proof memo:
  - what got replies
  - what got ignored
  - who moved toward a call
  - what should be repeated next week

## Execution Rules
- No broad segment expansion this week.
- No paid ads revival during this sprint.
- No new CTA experiments; keep the CTA call-led.
- No blind scale if bounce rate or reply quality is poor.

## Daily Operating Rhythm
- Morning: check replies, booked calls, and hot follow-ups
- Midday: source or qualify new leads
- Afternoon: send or adjust the current batch
- End of day: log outcomes and update Asana

## What Matters Most
The point of this week is not to prove that outbound works in general. The point is to prove that a university-specific offer, lead set, and follow-up path can create real conversations faster than the old broad motion.
