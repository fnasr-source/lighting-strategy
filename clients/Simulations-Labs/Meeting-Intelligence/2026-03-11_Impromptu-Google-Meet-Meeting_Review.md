- The working conclusion in the room was that ads are not sustainable for the current stage.
- Youssef confirmed the value from ads was mainly keyword learning for SEO, not repeatable acquisition.

### 2. The education segment is the clearest wedge
- Fouad and Moataz both pushed to narrow the focus to universities.
- The strongest live signal discussed in the meeting came from education conversations, especially university-related use cases.
- The team agreed universities are easier to explain, easier to get into with a pilot, and more aligned with current messaging than broader enterprise/security motions.

### 3. The immediate offer should be guided, not self-serve
- Repeated theme: the product is too complex for a cold self-serve trial CTA.
- The better CTA is not "sign up" but a guided pilot or discovery call.
- The offer needs to feel like a supported rollout for one cohort, not a software signup.

### 4. The account still lacks proof and structured follow-up
- Fouad called out that the team still cannot easily point to concrete education proof or named use cases.
- Interested leads are getting one-off replies or a guide, but not a persistent conversion sequence.
- Moataz explicitly challenged the team to follow interested contacts with tighter value-led email sequences, not just send a guide and stop.

### 5. The university ICP needs to be made more concrete
- Moataz kept pushing for the exact buyer/operator profile inside universities.
- The meeting showed that "educators" is still too broad.
- The better operating model is to split university outreach into decision makers, program owners, and practical operators.

## Key Decisions

### Decision 1: Focus on education now
For the next cycle, Simulations Labs should prioritize universities and education programs over other target segments.

### Decision 2: Keep the CTA call-led
The main conversion target should be a discovery call or pilot scoping call, not a direct product signup.

### Decision 3: Build the next outbound wave around universities
Apollo sourcing, email copy, and follow-up should be rewritten around university use cases and practical lab delivery.

### Decision 4: Tighten the post-reply flow
If a lead replies or requests a guide, the team needs a clearer follow-up sequence with proof, use case framing, and one next step.

### Decision 5: Create a smaller, clearer operating focus for the next 7 days
The meeting favored fewer moving parts and one main bet: universities.
