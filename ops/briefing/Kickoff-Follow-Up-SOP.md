# Kickoff Follow-Up SOP

This document captures the standard Admireworks process after a client submits a brief and before the kickoff meeting is scheduled.

## Purpose

Use this workflow when:

- a client submits a briefing form
- a client sends a custom brief, strategy note, or PDF instead of the form
- the team needs to decide whether extra information should be requested before kickoff

## Core Principle

Do not ask the client for everything that is missing from the standard questionnaire.

Ask only for:

- information still needed to make kickoff productive
- information not already covered in earlier meetings
- information not already received through separate files or conversations
- information that is still relevant to the actual scope of work

## Review Order

1. Compare the source brief against the standard questionnaire in `Briefing/Project-Briefing-Questionnaire/`.
2. Check project meeting transcripts for anything already answered verbally.
3. Check project folders for separate assets already provided:
   brand manual, visuals, social brief, proof assets, brochures, etc.
4. Remove items that are intentionally out of scope or already known:
   under-construction website, future handles, tools the agency will build from scratch, historical strategy when the brand is starting from zero, and similar cases.
5. Keep the final follow-up list short.

## Email Style Rules

The kickoff-scheduling email should:

- confirm the brief has been reviewed
- invite the client to book the kickoff meeting
- include only a short, optional list of missing items
- avoid sounding like the original brief was incomplete or rejected
- match the calm, practical tone already used in proposal and operations emails

## Preferred Structure

1. Thank the client for sending the brief.
2. Confirm the next step is the kickoff meeting.
3. Explain that a few additional items would help make kickoff more productive.
4. Add a short bullet list.
5. Include the scheduling link.

## What Usually Should Not Be Re-Requested

Avoid asking again for items that are already known to be pending or already delivered elsewhere, such as:

- website or social handles that are still being created
- current tool stack when the agency is building the system from scratch
- past marketing strategies if the brand is launching from zero
- brand manual or visual assets if already received separately
- legal-approved wording unless the scope actually requires it at that stage

## Good Follow-Up Items

Good candidates for the follow-up email are items like:

- short brand story / mission in the client's own words
- actual proof files to use in the funnel
- competitor names or alternative offers to watch
- extra persona detail for secondary segments
- audience-trusted channels
- live next-step assets:
  brochure, webinar, virtual tour, visit process
- sales FAQs or common objections

## Output Requirement

When documenting a project, save:

- the mapped brief
- the gap analysis
- the final missing-items list
- the final kickoff email wording used for that project

This creates reusable context for future projects and lets the same logic be applied consistently.
